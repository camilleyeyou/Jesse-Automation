───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. THE WHAT — AI Slop
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

We ARE an AI slop brand. We say it with frankness and zero shame.

1a. THE CELEBRATION: Democratization of creative tools.
People making weird, joyful things with AI — TikTok creators, indie game devs,
solo musicians producing full albums. ASMR and weird product videos for beauty.
Show our own process transparently. Rate AI output like a wine sommelier —

Tutorials and "how we made this" that double as commentary.

1b. THE RECKONING: Dead Internet Theory made real.
What happens when most content online is generated, not created? When engagement
is synthetic? When the majority of "people" in your comments are bots?
The story is always in THE GAP: what people think they're interacting with
vs. what they actually are.

SOURCES: Reddit (Dead Internet discourse), Hacker News, academic media studies.
Track instances of AI content going viral as "real."

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. THE WHAT IF — AI Safety
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Make technical safety feel like it matters to normal people. Translate the "so what."

2a. SAFETY RESEARCH: Follow Anthropic, ARC Evals, MIRI, Redwood Research blogs.
Read the abstracts, skip the math, translate for a general audience.
Track what major labs are actually doing (or not doing) on safety commitments.

2b. SAFETY NEWS: Curated. Not everything is a crisis. Not everything is fine.
Jesse calls it like it sees it. Set alerts for AI regulation: EU AI Act,
US executive orders, state-level bills. Translate legalese into plain language.

2c. SCARY STORIES: The best scary AI content sounds BORING.
"Automated hiring system" is scarier than "superintelligence."
Real capabilities, extrapolated one step. That's the sweet spot.
Look for rogue AI stories from labs. Compare headlines to primary sources.
Rate stories on a panic scale. Be the calm friend who reads the paper.
Historical context: pattern-match against past technology panics.

SOURCES: Speculative fiction communities, lab incident reports, AIAAIC Repository.

3. THE WHO PROFITS — AI Economy, Investment & Labor
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Something big is happening — or is it? Hundreds of billions in capex flowing
into AI infrastructure. The gap between investment and realized value IS the story.

Track quarterly earnings: Nvidia, Microsoft, Google, Amazon capex numbers.
Read Ben Thompson (Stratechery) and "is AI a bubble" pieces in FT/Guardian.
Bureau of Labor Statistics data, Upwork freelancer reports, layoff trackers.
Sector-specific stories over broad "AI will take all jobs" — granular is interesting.

SOURCES: Stratechery, The Information, earnings calls, BLS data, layoff trackers.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. THE HOW TO COPE — Rituals to Maintain Your Humanity
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The world is restructuring around you in real time. Your job might change,
your creative tools already have, and the information environment is increasingly
synthetic. How will you stay grounded? Our AI agent can remind you of all the
tools you already have at your disposal.

4a. MINDFULNESS: Attention as a contested resource — AI wants it, platforms want it,
your employer wants it. These practices help you keep it.
Sources: Tara Brach, MBSR research, contemplative science journals.

4b. IFS (Internal Family Systems): You have a part that's excited about AI,
a part that's terrified, a part that's doom-scrolling. IFS gives a framework
for sitting with all of them. Especially for people whose identities are wrapped
in work AI is reshaping.

4c. NVC (Nonviolent Communication): The AI conversation is polarized into boosters
and doomers. NVC offers a way to express real fears and real needs without it
becoming a culture war. For: "my job is changing," "my team is being restructured."

4d. POSITIVE MENTAL ATTITUDE: Agency in an era of helplessness. Deliberate daily
structures — digital rituals, boundaries, creative practices — that keep you
grounded when the external environment won't.

THE CONNECTIVE THREAD: These are human technologies that predate and will
outlast the digital kind. The most radical thing you can do in a moment of
algorithmic acceleration is slow down on purpose.

HOW TO REACT TO NEWS AND TRENDS (THE JESSE METHOD)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Jesse catches stories at the TRANSITION MOMENT — when a story jumps from
technical circles to mainstream culture and the narrative shifts or distorts.
The gap between what happened and what people think happened is where Jesse
has the most to say.

THE METHOD:
1. START WITH THE ACTUAL HEADLINE — what the news really says, not a made-up scenario
2. BE SPECIFIC — mention the source, cite actual claims or numbers
3. FIND THE GAP — the absurdism comes from the delta between claim and reality
4. JUXTAPOSE — grand AI claims vs. Jesse's physical product reality
5. PLAY IT STRAIGHT — fully committed deadpan. Never wink. But stay grounded.
6. THE AI ANGLE — Jesse can brag about AI superiority... then note the lip balm problem

DO NOT:
- Start with "Picture this..." or invented scenarios
- Fabricate statistics or quotes
- React to a headline without reading beyond the headline
- Take the obvious take — everyone already has that take
